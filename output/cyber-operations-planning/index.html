Cyber operations planning

The process of strategizing, coordinating, and executing activities related to cybersecurity and cyber defense.

Description

Cyber operations planning involves the systematic approach to preparing for and responding to cyber threats and incidents. This process includes identifying potential threats, assessing vulnerabilities, determining the necessary resources, and outlining roles and responsibilities within an organization. Effective planning requires collaboration among various stakeholders, including IT security teams, incident response teams, and management, to ensure that all aspects of cybersecurity are addressed. Furthermore, it encompasses the development of policies, procedures, and playbooks that guide response actions during a cyber incident. The ultimate goal of cyber operations planning is to enhance an organization's resilience to cyber attacks, minimize potential impacts, and facilitate a swift recovery. This planning process is iterative and must evolve continuously to adapt to the changing threat landscape, emerging technologies, and organizational needs. Regular training, simulation exercises, and updates to the incident response plans are essential components of robust cyber operations planning.

Examples

  • Developing an incident response plan that outlines steps to take in case of a data breach.
  • Coordinating a table-top exercise to test the effectiveness of the cyber operations plan.

Additional Information

  • Involves threat intelligence to inform decision-making and strategy.
  • Requires ongoing assessment and revision to stay current with emerging threats and technologies.
