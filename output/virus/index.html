Virus

A type of malicious software that replicates itself by infecting other programs and files.

Description

In the context of cybersecurity, a virus is a type of malware designed to replicate itself and spread from one computer to another. Viruses attach themselves to legitimate programs or files, and when these programs are executed, the virus is activated. This can lead to various harmful actions, such as corrupting or deleting files, stealing personal information, or using the infected device to spread the virus further. Unlike other types of malware, viruses require a host program to execute and spread, which differentiates them from worms that can propagate independently. Viruses can be transmitted through infected email attachments, downloads, or even removable media like USB drives. The impact of a virus can range from mild inconveniences, like slow performance, to severe consequences, including data loss and system failures. Effective antivirus software is essential for detecting and removing viruses, along with practicing safe browsing and email habits to prevent infection.

Examples

  • ILOVEYOU Virus: An email worm that spread rapidly in 2000, causing widespread damage.
  • Melissa Virus: A macro virus that infected Microsoft Word documents in 1999, spreading via email.

Additional Information

  • Viruses can often be prevented by keeping software updated and using reliable antivirus programs.
  • Education on safe computing practices is crucial in minimizing the risk of virus infections.
