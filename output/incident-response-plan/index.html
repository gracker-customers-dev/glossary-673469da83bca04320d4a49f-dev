Incident response plan

A structured approach to managing and addressing cybersecurity incidents.

Description

An incident response plan (IRP) is a documented strategy that outlines the processes and procedures an organization follows when a cybersecurity incident occurs. The primary goal of an IRP is to effectively manage the incident to minimize damage, reduce recovery time and costs, and mitigate the risk of future incidents. The plan typically includes preparation activities, detection and analysis of incidents, containment strategies, eradication of threats, recovery efforts, and post-incident activities such as lessons learned and updates to policies. A well-defined IRP helps ensure that all stakeholders understand their roles and responsibilities during an incident, promotes efficient communication, and enables quicker responses to threats. Regular testing and updating of the plan are crucial to adapt to evolving cyber threats and organizational changes, ensuring that the IRP remains effective and relevant.

Examples

  • A company develops an IRP that includes steps for identifying and reporting phishing attempts.
  • An organization conducts a tabletop exercise to simulate a data breach and test their incident response procedures.

Additional Information

  • An effective IRP should be reviewed and updated regularly to stay current with the threat landscape.
  • Training employees on the IRP is essential to ensure everyone knows how to respond in the event of an incident.
