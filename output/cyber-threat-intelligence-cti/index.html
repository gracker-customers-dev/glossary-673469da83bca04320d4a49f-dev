Cyber threat intelligence (CTI)

Information that helps organizations understand and mitigate cyber threats.

Description

Cyber threat intelligence (CTI) refers to the collection, analysis, and dissemination of information regarding potential or current cyber threats that can impact an organization’s security posture. CTI encompasses a broad range of data, including information about threat actors, their capabilities, intentions, and the tactics, techniques, and procedures (TTPs) they employ. This intelligence enables organizations to anticipate and respond proactively to cyber threats, enhancing their ability to defend against attacks. CTI can be categorized into strategic, tactical, operational, and technical intelligence, each serving different purposes. Strategic intelligence informs high-level decision-making, while tactical intelligence focuses on specific threats. Operational intelligence provides insights into ongoing attacks, and technical intelligence offers data about vulnerabilities and exploits. By leveraging CTI, organizations can improve their incident response times, enhance their risk management strategies, and prioritize their security investments based on the most relevant threats.

Examples

  • Threat intelligence reports from cybersecurity firms detailing emerging threats.
  • Indicators of compromise (IOCs) shared within threat intelligence communities.

Additional Information

  • CTI can be sourced from public, private, and government organizations.
  • Effective use of CTI requires integration with security operations and incident response teams.
