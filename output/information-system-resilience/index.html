Information system resilience

The ability of an information system to withstand and recover from adverse conditions, including cyberattacks and technical failures.

Description

Information system resilience refers to the capacity of an information system to prepare for, respond to, and recover from disruptive events, including cyber threats, hardware failures, and natural disasters. This resilience is critical in maintaining the availability, integrity, and confidentiality of information systems and the data they manage. A resilient information system implements various strategies such as redundancy, failover mechanisms, regular backups, and incident response plans. It also encompasses ongoing risk assessments and the ability to adapt to evolving threats. The goal is to ensure that the system can continue to function and provide services, even under adverse conditions, and can quickly return to normal operations after an incident. Resilience is not just about preventing failures but also about being able to manage and mitigate the impact of those failures when they occur.

Examples

  • A company implements a backup server that can take over if the primary server fails due to an attack.
  • An organization conducts regular training for employees on cybersecurity best practices to reduce the risk of human error.

Additional Information

  • Resilience involves both technical and organizational aspects, including policies and procedures.
  • Regular testing of incident response plans helps ensure preparedness for real-world cyber incidents.
