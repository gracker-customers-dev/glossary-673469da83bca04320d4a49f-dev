Phishing

A cyber attack that attempts to obtain sensitive information by masquerading as a trustworthy entity.

Description

Phishing is a type of cyber attack where attackers deceive individuals into providing sensitive information such as usernames, passwords, credit card numbers, and other personal details. This is typically done by posing as a legitimate organization through emails, text messages, or fake websites. The attackers often create a sense of urgency or use social engineering tactics to manipulate the target into taking immediate action, such as clicking a link or opening an attachment. Phishing attacks can take many forms, including spear phishing, which targets specific individuals or organizations, and whaling, which targets high-profile executives. The rise of digital communication has led to an increase in phishing attacks, making it crucial for individuals and organizations to be aware of the techniques used by attackers and to implement security measures such as email filtering, user education, and multi-factor authentication to mitigate risks.

Examples

  • An email that appears to be from a bank asking the recipient to confirm their account information by clicking on a link.
  • A text message that claims to be from a delivery service requesting personal details to reschedule a delivery.

Additional Information

  • Phishing attacks can also occur via social media and phone calls, referred to as vishing (voice phishing).
  • Regular training and awareness programs are essential in helping individuals recognize and avoid phishing attempts.
