Traffic light protocol

A system for sharing sensitive information in cybersecurity that uses color codes to indicate the level of sharing permitted.

Description

The Traffic Light Protocol (TLP) is a standardized framework used in cybersecurity to facilitate the sharing of sensitive information among individuals and organizations. It employs a color-coding system to indicate the sharing restrictions associated with the information being shared. There are four colors in TLP: Red, Amber, Green, and White. Each color represents a different level of sharing: Red indicates that the information is strictly confidential and should only be shared with specific individuals; Amber suggests that the information can be shared with a limited audience on a need-to-know basis; Green implies that the information can be shared freely within the community; and White indicates that the information can be shared publicly. This protocol enhances communication between organizations, especially during incidents, by clarifying how and with whom the information can be shared, thus improving the overall security posture of the involved parties.

Examples

  • A cybersecurity firm shares a vulnerability report with a TLP: Amber label, restricting its distribution to trusted partners.
  • A government agency issues a warning about a cyber threat with a TLP: Red label, indicating that only specific officials should access the information.

Additional Information

  • TLP is widely used in various sectors, including government, private industry, and international organizations.
  • The protocol helps to prevent unauthorized dissemination of sensitive information and promotes responsible information sharing.
