Computer network defense infrastructure support

The systems and processes that protect, monitor, and manage the security of computer networks.

Description

Computer network defense infrastructure support refers to the comprehensive framework that encompasses various technologies, processes, and personnel aimed at defending computer networks against cyber threats. This infrastructure includes firewalls, intrusion detection systems (IDS), intrusion prevention systems (IPS), and security information and event management (SIEM) systems, among others. These components work together to monitor network traffic for suspicious activities, prevent unauthorized access, and respond to security incidents. Effective network defense also relies on policies and procedures that govern the use of these technologies, as well as continuous monitoring and updating of security measures to adapt to evolving threats. Regular training and awareness programs for personnel are also crucial to ensure that everyone involved understands their role in maintaining network security. In a constantly changing cyber landscape, having a robust defense infrastructure is essential for safeguarding sensitive data and maintaining the overall integrity and availability of networked systems.

Examples

  • Firewalls that filter incoming and outgoing network traffic based on predefined security rules.
  • Intrusion Detection Systems (IDS) that alert administrators to potential threats on the network.

Additional Information

  • Regular updates and patches are necessary to keep defense systems effective against new vulnerabilities.
  • Collaboration between IT and cybersecurity teams enhances the overall security posture of the organization.
