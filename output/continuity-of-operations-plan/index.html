Continuity of operations plan

A strategy to ensure that essential functions continue during and after a disaster.

Description

A Continuity of Operations Plan (COOP) is a comprehensive strategy developed by an organization to ensure that its essential functions can continue during and after a significant disruption, such as a cyber attack, natural disaster, or other emergencies. In the context of cybersecurity, COOP is vital for protecting critical data and maintaining operational integrity. The plan outlines procedures for sustaining and recovering key operations, including personnel responsibilities, communication protocols, and resource allocations. It typically includes risk assessments, impact analyses, and strategies for maintaining IT systems and data security. Regular testing and updates to the COOP are necessary to adapt to new threats and changes in the organizational structure. A well-prepared COOP helps minimize downtime, safeguard sensitive information, and maintain trust with stakeholders during crises.

Examples

  • An organization implements a COOP to ensure their online services remain operational during a cyber attack.
  • A government agency develops a COOP to maintain communication and service delivery during a natural disaster.

Additional Information

  • COOPs should be regularly reviewed and updated to reflect changes in technology and threats.
  • Training and exercises are essential to ensure staff are familiar with the plan and can execute it effectively during an emergency.
