Cyber infrastructure

The integrated hardware, software, and network resources that support the delivery of digital services and data.

Description

Cyber infrastructure encompasses the foundational technological components that enable the functioning of computer networks and systems. This includes physical hardware such as servers, routers, and switches, as well as software applications, operating systems, and security protocols that protect data and systems. The term also refers to the interconnected networks that allow for data exchange and communication between various devices and systems. In the context of cybersecurity, cyber infrastructure is vital for ensuring the security, availability, and integrity of information. Organizations must invest in robust cyber infrastructure to defend against cyber threats, ensure compliance with regulatory requirements, and maintain business continuity. A well-designed cyber infrastructure can help organizations mitigate risks associated with data breaches, cyberattacks, and other vulnerabilities by implementing security measures such as firewalls, intrusion detection systems, and encryption. Overall, effective cyber infrastructure is essential for supporting the operational needs of modern organizations while safeguarding their digital assets.

Examples

  • Data centers that host critical applications and services.
  • Cloud computing platforms that provide scalable resources for businesses.

Additional Information

  • Includes both physical and virtual components.
  • Is a key focus area for cybersecurity strategy and risk management.
