Industrial control system

A type of computer system used to control industrial processes and infrastructure.

Description

Industrial control systems (ICS) are integrated systems that monitor and control physical processes in industrial environments. These systems are crucial for managing operations in sectors such as manufacturing, energy, water treatment, and transportation. ICS includes various components such as supervisory control and data acquisition (SCADA) systems, distributed control systems (DCS), and programmable logic controllers (PLC). They operate in real-time to ensure safety, efficiency, and reliability in production and infrastructure management. ICS are often connected to larger networks, which makes them vulnerable to cybersecurity threats, including unauthorized access, malware, and cyberattacks that could disrupt operations or cause physical damage. As industries increasingly adopt IoT and smart technologies, the security of ICS has become paramount, necessitating robust protective measures and compliance with standards such as NIST, ISA/IEC, and others. The convergence of IT and OT (Operational Technology) further complicates security, as traditional cybersecurity measures may not be sufficient to protect these critical systems.

Examples

  • Supervisory Control and Data Acquisition (SCADA) systems used in water treatment plants.
  • Programmable Logic Controllers (PLCs) used in manufacturing assembly lines.

Additional Information

  • ICS can be vulnerable to cyberattacks that may lead to physical consequences.
  • Cybersecurity frameworks like NIST Cybersecurity Framework provide guidelines for securing ICS.
