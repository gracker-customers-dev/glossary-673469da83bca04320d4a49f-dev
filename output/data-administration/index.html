Data administration

The process of managing and overseeing data resources to ensure data integrity, security, and accessibility.

Description

Data administration refers to the systematic management of data resources within an organization to ensure their accuracy, availability, and security. In the context of cybersecurity, data administration plays a crucial role in protecting sensitive information from unauthorized access, loss, or corruption. It involves defining data management policies, establishing data governance frameworks, and implementing access controls and security measures to safeguard data against threats. Effective data administration ensures that data is stored securely, backed up regularly, and compliant with relevant regulations. It also includes monitoring data usage and maintaining data quality through regular audits and assessments. As organizations increasingly rely on data for decision-making and operational efficiency, robust data administration practices become essential to mitigate risks and enhance data security.

Examples

  • Implementing role-based access control to restrict data access based on user roles.
  • Regularly conducting data audits to identify and rectify discrepancies in data records.

Additional Information

  • Data administration is often part of a larger data governance strategy.
  • It requires collaboration between IT, security, and business units to align data management practices with organizational goals.
