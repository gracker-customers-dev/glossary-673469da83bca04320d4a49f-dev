Intrusion

Unauthorized access or breach of a computer system or network.

Description

In the context of cybersecurity, an intrusion refers to any unauthorized attempt to access, manipulate, or damage a computer system or network. This can encompass a wide range of malicious activities, including hacking, malware installation, data theft, and exploitation of vulnerabilities. Intrusions can occur from external sources, such as cybercriminals or state-sponsored attackers, or from internal sources, such as disgruntled employees or careless users. The consequences of an intrusion can be severe, leading to data breaches, loss of sensitive information, financial loss, and damage to an organization's reputation. To combat intrusions, organizations deploy various security measures, including firewalls, intrusion detection systems (IDS), and encryption. Regular monitoring and updating of security protocols are essential to protect against evolving threats. Effective incident response plans are also crucial for minimizing the impact of an intrusion when it does occur.

Examples

  • A hacker gains unauthorized access to a corporate network to steal sensitive customer data.
  • Malware is deployed to exploit a vulnerability in a software application, allowing attackers to control the affected systems.

Additional Information

  • Intrusions can be detected through monitoring tools that analyze network traffic for suspicious activity.
  • Organizations often conduct penetration testing to identify and remediate vulnerabilities that could lead to intrusions.
