Intrusion detection

The process of monitoring network or system activities for malicious activities or policy violations.

Description

Intrusion detection is a critical component of cybersecurity that involves the identification of unauthorized access or anomalies within a network or system. It is achieved through the deployment of Intrusion Detection Systems (IDS), which analyze traffic patterns and system behavior in real-time or through periodic scans. The primary goal of intrusion detection is to detect potential threats before they can cause significant harm, allowing organizations to respond swiftly to incidents. Intrusion detection can be categorized into two main types: network-based IDS (NIDS) that monitor network traffic for suspicious activities and host-based IDS (HIDS) that analyze the activity on individual devices. Additionally, intrusion detection can also be classified as signature-based, which detects known threats based on predefined signatures, or anomaly-based, which identifies deviations from established baselines of normal behavior. Effective intrusion detection helps organizations improve their overall security posture and compliance with regulatory requirements by providing visibility into potential security breaches.

Examples

  • An IDS alerts administrators when it detects unusual login attempts from an unknown IP address.
  • A network-based IDS identifies and logs a potential DoS attack by monitoring traffic spikes.

Additional Information

  • Intrusion detection systems can be integrated with other security measures like firewalls and SIEM systems.
  • Regular updates and tuning of IDS configurations are essential to maintain effectiveness against evolving threats.
