Passive attack

A type of cyber attack where the attacker monitors or collects data without altering or interfering with the system.

Description

In cybersecurity, a passive attack refers to an unauthorized attempt to access or intercept data without altering it. Unlike active attacks, where an attacker tries to disrupt or manipulate the system, passive attacks focus solely on eavesdropping or gathering information, often to exploit it later. These attacks can occur in various forms, such as network traffic analysis, where attackers capture data packets traveling over a network to glean sensitive information like passwords, credit card numbers, or personal messages. Passive attacks can be particularly challenging to detect because they do not leave obvious traces or alter the system's behavior. Common methods include wiretapping, session hijacking, and monitoring unsecured Wi-Fi networks. Although passive attacks do not directly harm the system, the information gained can lead to more severe threats, including identity theft or targeted active attacks. Organizations must implement strong encryption, secure communication protocols, and vigilant monitoring to defend against such threats.

Examples

  • Network traffic sniffing to capture sensitive data.
  • Eavesdropping on unsecured Wi-Fi networks to gather login credentials.

Additional Information

  • Passive attacks are often difficult to detect.
  • Encryption can help mitigate the risks associated with passive attacks.
