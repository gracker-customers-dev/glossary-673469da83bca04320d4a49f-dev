Integrity

The assurance that information is accurate, consistent, and trustworthy over its entire lifecycle.

Description

In the context of cybersecurity, integrity refers to the protection of data from unauthorized modification or destruction. It ensures that information remains unchanged and uncorrupted during storage, processing, and transmission. Integrity is a crucial aspect of information security, as it helps maintain the reliability and accuracy of data, which is essential for decision-making processes, legal compliance, and operational effectiveness. Various measures, such as checksums, hashes, and digital signatures, are employed to verify data integrity. These methods help detect any unauthorized changes to the data and can alert administrators to potential security breaches. Maintaining data integrity is vital not only for individual users and organizations but also for systems that rely on accurate data for functioning, such as financial institutions, healthcare providers, and government agencies. Without integrity, the trustworthiness of data is compromised, leading to potentially severe consequences, including financial loss, damage to reputation, and legal repercussions.

Examples

  • Using cryptographic hash functions to ensure files have not been altered.
  • Implementing access controls to prevent unauthorized users from modifying sensitive data.

Additional Information

  • Integrity is one of the three core principles of the CIA triad (Confidentiality, Integrity, Availability).
  • Data integrity issues can arise from both accidental errors and malicious attacks, such as malware or insider threats.
