Operational exercise

A simulated activity designed to evaluate and improve cybersecurity readiness and response capabilities.

Description

An operational exercise in the context of cybersecurity is a structured, scenario-based activity that aims to test an organization's incident response capabilities, decision-making processes, and overall readiness to handle cyber threats. These exercises can range from tabletop drills, where participants discuss responses to hypothetical incidents, to full-scale simulations involving real-time responses to cyber attacks. During these exercises, various teams, including IT, cybersecurity, and management, collaborate to identify strengths and weaknesses in their current protocols and to enhance their ability to respond effectively to potential incidents. Operational exercises also provide valuable training opportunities, allowing participants to familiarize themselves with tools, technologies, and communication strategies that will be crucial during actual incidents. The insights gained from these exercises are essential for developing a robust cybersecurity posture and ensuring that organizations can minimize the impact of cyber threats on their operations.

Examples

  • A tabletop exercise simulating a ransomware attack on the organization's network.
  • A live-fire exercise that tests the response to a simulated data breach in real-time.

Additional Information

  • Operational exercises can involve external stakeholders, such as law enforcement or incident response teams.
  • Regularly conducting these exercises is recommended to keep skills sharp and to adapt to evolving cyber threats.
