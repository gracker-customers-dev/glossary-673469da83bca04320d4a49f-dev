Operations technology

Operations technology refers to the hardware and software systems used to monitor and control physical processes in industrial settings.

Description

Operations technology (OT) encompasses the tools and technologies that manage and optimize the operations of machinery and physical processes, particularly in sectors like manufacturing, energy, and utilities. Unlike traditional information technology (IT), which focuses on data management and enterprise applications, OT is concerned with the operational aspects that ensure systems run efficiently and safely. OT includes systems such as Supervisory Control and Data Acquisition (SCADA) systems, Distributed Control Systems (DCS), and Industrial Control Systems (ICS). In the context of cybersecurity, OT systems are often targeted due to their critical roles in infrastructure and production processes. The convergence of IT and OT has created new challenges and opportunities, as organizations seek to integrate these systems while ensuring robust cybersecurity measures are in place to protect against threats that could disrupt operations or cause physical damage.

Examples

  • SCADA systems used in power plants to monitor and control electricity generation.
  • Industrial control systems used in manufacturing to automate production lines.

Additional Information

  • OT is becoming increasingly interconnected with IT systems, raising cybersecurity concerns.
  • Ensuring the security of OT systems is critical to safeguarding national infrastructure and industrial operations.
