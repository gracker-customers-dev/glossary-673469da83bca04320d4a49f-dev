Cyber incident response plan

A structured approach for preparing for, detecting, responding to, and recovering from cybersecurity incidents.

Description

A Cyber Incident Response Plan (CIRP) is a documented strategy that outlines the procedures and protocols an organization must follow when dealing with a cybersecurity incident. This plan ensures that the organization can effectively manage incidents that threaten its information systems, data integrity, and overall cybersecurity posture. A well-structured CIRP includes identification and assessment of potential threats, roles and responsibilities of the incident response team, communication strategies, and steps for containment, eradication, and recovery. Additionally, it typically involves training and awareness programs for staff, ongoing testing and updating of the plan, and a post-incident review to learn from the incident and improve future responses. The goal of a CIRP is to minimize the impact of incidents on operations and to restore normal functions as quickly and efficiently as possible, while also ensuring compliance with regulatory requirements and maintaining public trust.

Examples

  • A company uses a CIRP to respond to a ransomware attack by isolating affected systems and restoring data from backups.
  • An organization conducts regular drills based on its CIRP to prepare its staff for potential phishing attacks.

Additional Information

  • Cyber incident response plans should be regularly reviewed and updated to adapt to evolving threats.
  • Collaboration with law enforcement and cybersecurity experts can enhance the effectiveness of a CIRP during significant incidents.
