Operate & maintain

The processes involved in the ongoing management and upkeep of cybersecurity systems.

Description

In the context of cybersecurity, 'Operate & maintain' refers to the continuous processes and practices that ensure the effective functioning of cybersecurity systems and measures. This includes the routine monitoring, management, and updating of security solutions such as firewalls, intrusion detection systems, and antivirus programs. The objective is to protect an organization's information systems from potential threats and vulnerabilities. This phase encompasses not only the technical operations of security tools but also the development of policies and protocols that guide the use of these tools. Regular maintenance activities typically involve applying patches, conducting vulnerability assessments, and ensuring compliance with security standards. Additionally, incident response plans are tested and updated to prepare for potential security breaches. Overall, effective operation and maintenance are crucial for sustaining a robust cybersecurity posture, adapting to evolving threats, and ensuring organizational resilience.

Examples

  • Regularly updating antivirus software to protect against new threats.
  • Conducting monthly vulnerability scans to identify and mitigate security risks.

Additional Information

  • Involves collaboration between IT and cybersecurity teams for effective incident response.
  • Often requires training staff on security best practices and policies.
