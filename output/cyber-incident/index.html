Cyber incident

An event that compromises the confidentiality, integrity, or availability of computer systems or data.

Description

A cyber incident refers to any event that has an adverse effect on the cybersecurity posture of an organization. This can include a wide range of situations, from successful breaches where unauthorized access is gained to networks and sensitive data, to denial-of-service attacks that disrupt service availability. Cyber incidents can also encompass malware infections, data breaches, insider threats, and even the exploitation of vulnerabilities within software or hardware. The impact of such incidents can vary significantly, ranging from minor disruptions to severe financial and reputational damage. Organizations must have incident response plans in place to detect, respond to, and recover from these events to mitigate risks and protect their assets. The growing sophistication of cyber threats necessitates continuous monitoring, threat intelligence, and proactive measures to defend against potential incidents.

Examples

  • A ransomware attack encrypting critical files and demanding payment for decryption.
  • An unauthorized access incident where sensitive customer data is stolen by hackers.

Additional Information

  • Cyber incidents can lead to legal ramifications and regulatory penalties.
  • Effective incident response can significantly reduce the overall impact of a cyber incident.
