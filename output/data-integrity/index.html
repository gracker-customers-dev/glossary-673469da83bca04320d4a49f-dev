Data integrity

The accuracy and consistency of data over its lifecycle.

Description

Data integrity refers to the accuracy, completeness, and reliability of data stored in a database or data warehouse. It is a critical aspect of cybersecurity, as maintaining data integrity ensures that information is trustworthy and can be relied upon for decision-making processes. Data integrity can be compromised by unauthorized access, data corruption, or accidental deletion, which can lead to significant issues for organizations. There are two main types of data integrity: physical integrity, which safeguards data against physical issues such as hardware failures, and logical integrity, which ensures that data remains consistent and valid according to defined rules and constraints. Techniques such as checksums, hashing, and data validation are often employed to maintain data integrity and detect any alterations or corruption. Regular backups and data recovery plans are also essential to mitigate risks associated with data loss or corruption. Overall, ensuring data integrity is vital for the confidentiality, integrity, and availability (CIA) triad, which is foundational to effective cybersecurity practices.

Examples

  • Using checksums to verify data transfer integrity.
  • Implementing access controls to prevent unauthorized data modifications.

Additional Information

  • Data integrity is essential for compliance with regulations like GDPR and HIPAA.
  • Data integrity issues can lead to financial losses and damage to reputation.
