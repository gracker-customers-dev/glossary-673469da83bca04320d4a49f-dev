Data breach

An incident where unauthorized access is gained to sensitive, protected, or confidential data.

Description

A data breach occurs when an individual or group gains unauthorized access to confidential information, typically held by organizations, government entities, or individuals. This can involve the exposure, theft, or misuse of personal identifiable information (PII), financial data, health records, or any other sensitive material. Data breaches can happen through various means, including hacking, phishing attacks, accidental loss, or insider threats. The consequences of a data breach can be severe, leading to financial losses, reputational damage, legal ramifications, and loss of customer trust. Organizations are increasingly investing in cybersecurity measures to prevent data breaches, including encryption, firewalls, intrusion detection systems, and employee training. However, as technology evolves, so do the tactics employed by cybercriminals, making it crucial for organizations to remain vigilant and proactive in their security efforts.

Examples

  • The Equifax data breach of 2017, which exposed the personal information of 147 million people.
  • The Target data breach in 2013, where credit card information of over 40 million customers was compromised.

Additional Information

  • Data breaches can result in identity theft, leading to fraudulent activities.
  • Regulatory frameworks like GDPR and CCPA impose strict penalties on organizations that fail to protect personal data.
